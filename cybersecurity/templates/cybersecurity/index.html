    As technology develops and integrates deeper into our lives, the importance for strong cyber security awareness and practices grows. Our reliance on technology can introduce many risks; what would happen if hackers were able to disrupt our pacemakers, cars or banking systems?


    Cyber security is a field that looks at protecting the confidentiality, integrity, availability and irrefutability of information transmitted or stored by technology. We can investigate the vulnerabilities and threats that face technology, the implications of implementing poor security practices, and ways in which we can preserve the privacy of individual users.

    While awareness and implementation of strong ICT security practices is increasing, so are the volume and sophistication of cyber threats. Cyber security incidents are very common, and can affect everyone from businesses and government agencies to individuals. The Australian Crime Commission estimates put the annual cost of cyber crime to Australia at $1 billion a year. Tens of thousands of cyber security related incidents are reported to authorities across Australia each year, with these reports being used to develop a better idea of the threats facing Australia.

    Adding security to technology is not something that should be 'added on' on top, but rather needs to be built-in during design and development stages. Making technology secure is not a trivial process, but is greatly aided through user education and awareness.
