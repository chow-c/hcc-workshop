Cyber Security PHISHING

But wait, how are users so easily manipulated? Are people really the weakest link in the chain?

One of the main ways in which humans are exploited to grant malicious adversaries access to a system is through phishing emails. Phishing (pronounced fishing) is a form of social engineering, whereby people are manipulated into doing something without actually intending to. These types of cyber attacks exploit the nuances in human nature, such as our desires, our personality traits or our general willingness to help others.

Have you ever experienced a scam? Maybe a dodgy phone call or an online advertisement saying you've won a prize. Phishing is a special type of scam sent to you via an email, whereby a malicious actor is enticing you to click on a link or open an attachment. By doing so, you often fall victim to a cyber attack, in which the adversary can access your computer without your consent.

While some technical measures exist to help users detect phishing emails, often it comes down to a person's own judgement as to whether or not an email is legitimate.
