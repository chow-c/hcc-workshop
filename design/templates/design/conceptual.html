    Conceptual Models

    The last design principle is one of the most important, because it integrates all of the design principles Conceptual models are how we think things works. They are mental models that help useres understand the system and how to use it. Bad design of an interface can mess with the conceptual model and therefore its use!     Sticking to what people are used to

    When designing an interface it is really important to take into consideration what people probably already know. A lot of how people interact with an interface comes from their experiences or what they have been taught. This is called knowledge in the head! Knowledge in the head can be powerful when they build conceptual models of how a system or interface should work. That is why a lot of digital objects are similar to the real world.

    Another way of looking at it is that when designing interfaces one should stick to standards. These standards may be imposed by real world objects or they may be standards set by other systems. Going against standards that people have already learnt and know means it can be quite difficult for them to figure out how to use an interface. Take for example the clock below, can you tell the time?

    The clock doesn't conform to what your conceptual model of a clock is, it doesn't conform to the standard clock you learnt and it is therefore difficult for you to tell the time. The trick is that the numbers and hands are going in the opposite direction!
