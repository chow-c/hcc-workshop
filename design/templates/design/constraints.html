    Constraints

    Constraints are a type of feedback, that provide cues and limit the set of possible operations. Constraints are necessary for indicating proper use of a system.

    Affordances give STRONG cues on use

    If affordances are thought of as determining what action is possible, then signifiers are the signal for what the action is. An examples is, if a button must be pushed, the signifier is the text on the button signalling what action the push is, such as “Delete” or “Enter”. Perceived affordances act as signifiers.
