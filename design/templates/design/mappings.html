    Mappings

    Mappings are the link between what a control does and what you perceive it to do. Good mapping take advantage of the real world, of spacial and temporal analogies, so that users can easily work out what controls do what. An example that is often used is that of a light switch. Take a look at the light switch below, turn on the lights labeled A and C.

    A

    How hard did you think it was to find the right switch? If you're like most people you would have found it quite difficult and frustrating! That's because there is no logical mapping between the switches and the bulbs. No pattern in the mappings means increased time to learn which switch is for which bulb, and people have to learn from making mistakes. The best mappings are implied, making use of patterns and spatial reasoning.
