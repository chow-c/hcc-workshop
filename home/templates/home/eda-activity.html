    Using the Empatica E4 for EDA

    In this activity, you will be able to use an Empatica E4 on yourself in order to visualise your EDA activity.

    Initial Setup

    You will need:

    • An iPad
    • An Empatica E4
    A number of these devices will be available at the front of the room. Feel free to grab both an iPad and an E4 and take it back to your workstation.

    There are only a limited number of devices available, so you may need to attempt another activity in the meantime.

    You can complete this activity individually. You can also work with your colleagues in order to compare how your heart rate activity might be different to theirs.

    Putting the E4 on

    Pairing the devices

    Once the Empatica E4 is firmly secured to your wrist, you need to pair the device to the iPad.

    1. Launch the Empatica Realtime application

      Launch the application by touching the EmpaticaRT icon on the iPad. It should be on the third home screen.

    2. Prepare the E4

      Hold down the button on the Empatica E4 for 3 seconds. When a light turns on, this indicates that the E4 is in pairing mode.

    3. Start A New Session

      Touch Start A New Session. The app will now search for nearby devices in pairing mode. Select the Empatica E4 device as it appears in the selection list. If nothing happens, ensure the iPad's bluetooth is turned on.

    4. Off you go!

      You are paired and all set.

    Viewing your EDA

    You can now see your heart rate being tracked in real time.

    Attempt fluctuations

    Let's see if we can trick your sympathetic nervous system into changing your electrodermal activity.

    Observe your EDA on the iPad. Take a note of its current level, then watch the following video.

    Compare your data

    Take a note of your EDA levels, and compare it to one of your colleagues. Are there any major differences in your EDA and theirs?
