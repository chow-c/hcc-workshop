    Evaluating eReader interfaces

    In this activity, you will be able to compare a variety of different eReaders to evaluate the pros and cons of various interface features.

    Getting started

    In this activity you will perform a simple evaluation of a user interface of some eReaders. Although it is a simple evaluation, it demonstrates some key concepts:

    • this is a moderated user study - a technique widely used in UX research.
    • you will compare 2 devices - this is like A/B testing, a key technique used to compare different interfaces.
    This activity will also give you the opportunity to be an experimenter and a participant!

    This is a simplified version of user study that ANU researchers conducted. The study was conducted over several years and compared 20+ eReaders.

    Initial Setup

    You will need:

    • An eReader
    A number of eReaders will be available at the front of the room. Feel free to grab one and take it back to your workstation.

    There are only a limited number of devices available, so if there are no devices free, you may need to attempt another activity in the meantime.

    You can complete this activity individually. You can also work with your friends in order to compare multiple devices simultaneously. You could even take turns being researchers and participants.
