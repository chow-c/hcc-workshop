    The Thatcher Effect

    The Thatcher Effect, or Thatcher illusion, illustrates that the brain has special processes for face recognition, which do not apply when viewing upside down photo of a face. This has been illustrated by making local changes to the face, typically the eyes and mouth. Whilst the changes are blatantly obvious when the photo of the face is the right way up, and holistic face processing occurs, these changes are far more difficult to detect when the image is turned upside down.

    The effect is named after Margaret Thatcher, a former British Prime Minister, because her photo that was used to famously illustrate the illusion.
